About Me

I have many imaging science domain-related interests, but for the most part these boil down to remote sensing, computer vision, and modeling and simulation of imaging systems. I'm lucky to have had a background in both remote sensing for Earth observation and astronomical imaging applications - fitting for my love of space! I have a passion for creative computing solutions, and I keep up on accelerated and distributed computing concepts in my personal time. I'm happiest when I'm working on open ended problems and R&D! Outside of my professional life I spend time with my Shiba Inu Roxy, play a bunch of sports (hockey, volleyball, soccer, etc.), enjoy the outdoors and roadtrips, and love hacking on things with friends.

I'm very responsive, so feel free to shoot me a message or an email anytime! My phone number, address, and other personal data are not published online, however they are available on my private resume if required and relevant.

    ImagePypelines

    Project Page Home Page

    One of three lead developers of the ImagePypelines Python library. The library provides scientists and engineers with an easy interface for code reuse and non-sequential data processing pipeline refactoring, while abstracting logging, debugging, and workflow visualization. The library also allows scientists to remotely monitor their experiments and interact with live python processes on various connected hosts via a dashboard.
